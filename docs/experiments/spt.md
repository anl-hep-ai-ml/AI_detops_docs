# SPT-3G

The South Pole Telescope (SPT) is a 10-metre telescope at the Amundsen–Scott South Pole Station, designed to survey the Cosmic Microwave Background and detect galaxy clusters via the Sunyaev-Zel'dovich effect. The SPT-3G receiver deploys O(10,000) transition-edge sensor (TES) bolometers read out at ~200 Hz per channel.

## Application

Anomaly detection is applied to the continuous bolometer readout time-streams for detector health monitoring and data quality flagging. Anomaly types of interest include cosmic ray glitches, SQUID lockpoint failures, and correlated thermal noise events.

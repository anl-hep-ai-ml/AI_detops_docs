# Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) extends a language model by connecting it to an external knowledge store. Rather than relying solely on what the model learned during training, RAG retrieves relevant documents or data at inference time and supplies them as context. This is especially valuable in scientific settings where domain knowledge is specialised, frequently updated, or too voluminous to fit into a model's training data.

In detector operations, RAG could enable a system to answer questions grounded in experiment-specific documentation, run-condition logs, or detector calibration records.
